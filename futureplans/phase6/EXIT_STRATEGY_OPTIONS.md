# Exit Strategy Options (Month 24+)

**Context:** $20M ARR, 25K customers, 200 employees, profitable path by Month 30  
**Timeline:** Month 24 decision point (Feb 2028)  
**Founder Stake:** 40% (after Seed + Series A + Series B dilution)

---

## Option 1: IPO (Initial Public Offering)

### Target Timeline
**Month 36 (Feb 2029):** File S-1  
**Month 40 (June 2029):** Roadshow + IPO  
**Valuation:** $5-10B (10-20x ARR multiple for growth SaaS)

### Requirements

**Financial:**
- $100M ARR (Month 36 target)
- 3 consecutive quarters profitable (Q2-Q4 2028)
- 50%+ YoY growth (sustainable)
- >80% gross margin (software scalability)

**Operational:**
- SOC 2 Type II ✅ (Month 16)
- ISO 27001 ✅ (Month 22)
- FedRAMP (Month 30, government sales)
- Public company readiness (CFO, audit committee, Sarbanes-Oxley)

**Market:**
- $500M+ valuation minimum (Nasdaq/NYSE listing requirement)
- Comparable IPOs: GitLab ($15B, 2021), Confluent ($10B, 2021), HashiCorp ($15B, 2021)

### Pros
- **Liquidity:** Founders can sell 10-20% at IPO ($500M-1B at $5B valuation)
- **Currency:** Stock for acquisitions (buy competitors, consolidate market)
- **Brand:** Public company = enterprise credibility (Fortune 100 sales)
- **Employee Retention:** Stock options liquid (attract/retain talent)

### Cons
- **Quarterly Pressure:** Wall Street expectations (growth at all costs)
- **Disclosure:** Public financials, competitive insights exposed
- **Cost:** $10M+ (legal, banking, compliance)
- **Distraction:** 6-month process (founders spend 50% time on roadshow)

### Founder Outcome (40% stake)
- $5B valuation → $2B founder equity → $200M liquid at IPO (10% sell)
- Remaining $1.8B vests over 4 years (lock-up + quiet period)

## Option 3: Strategic Acquisition (Month 24-30)

### Potential Acquirers & Valuations

#### Tier 1: Tech Giants ($3-5B)

**Microsoft**
- Rationale: Azure DevOps, GitHub synergy (70M developers)
- Offer: $3-5B (15-25x ARR multiple)
- Structure: Cash + stock (60/40 split)
- Retention: 3-year earnout ($500M tied to integration)
- Precedent: GitHub ($7.5B, 2018), Nuance ($20B, 2021)

**Google**
- Rationale: Google Cloud, Android Studio integration
- Offer: $3-4B (similar to Microsoft)
- Structure: All stock (Alphabet shares)
- Risk: Integration into Google Cloud (loss of independence)

**Amazon**
- Rationale: AWS DevOps, CodeWhisperer (AI code assistant)
- Offer: $2-3B (lower multiple, AWS-first strategy)
- Structure: Cash (preferred for founders)
- Risk: Shut down standalone product, AWS-only

#### Tier 2: Dev Tools Companies ($1-3B)

**GitHub**
- Rationale: Marketplace leader, 100M developers
- Offer: $2-3B (10-15x ARR)
- Structure: Microsoft stock (GitHub = MSFT subsidiary)
- Integration: ODAVL as GitHub Actions native feature
- Precedent: Semmle ($100M, 2019), npm ($10M, 2020)

**GitLab**
- Rationale: DevOps platform (CI/CD + code quality)
- Offer: $1-2B (5-10x ARR, GitLab stock)
- Structure: Stock swap (less attractive, GitLab public but volatile)
- Integration: GitLab Ultimate tier (code quality expansion)

**JetBrains**
- Rationale: IDE leader (IntelliJ, PyCharm, WebStorm)
- Offer: $1-1.5B (privately held, cash-rich)
- Structure: Cash (clean exit, no earnout)
- Integration: Native IDE plugin (IntelliJ, PyCharm built-in)
- Precedent: No public acquisitions (JetBrains historically organic)

#### Tier 3: PE Firms / Growth Equity ($500M-1B)

**Vista Equity Partners**
- Rationale: SaaS rollup strategy (buy, optimize, exit)
- Offer: $500M-1B (2-5x ARR, depends on profitability)
- Structure: Cash + earnout (50/50)
- Timeline: 3-5 year hold, IPO or secondary sale
- Precedent: Marketo ($1.8B), Tibco ($4.3B)

**Thoma Bravo**
- Rationale: Enterprise software specialist
- Offer: $750M-1.5B (similar to Vista)
- Structure: Leverage buyout (debt + equity)
- Risk: Cut costs aggressively (layoffs, product focus narrow)

### Negotiation Strategy

**Step 1: Inbound Interest (Month 24-25)**
- Acquirers reach out (GitHub, MSFT likely first)
- NDA + initial call (no commitment)
- Share high-level metrics (ARR, growth, customers)

**Step 2: Banker Engagement (Month 25)**
- Hire M&A advisor (Qatalyst, LionTree, Moelis)
- Fee: 1-2% of deal value ($20-50M on $2-3B deal)
- Create "book" (investor deck for strategic buyers)

**Step 3: Auction Process (Month 26-27)**
- Share data room (financials, customers, product roadmap)
- First round bids (non-binding, valuation range)
- Narrow to 2-3 finalists (highest bid + best fit)

**Step 4: Management Presentations (Month 27)**
- Founders present to CEO/Board of acquirer
- Product demo, technical deep-dive, integration plan
- Due diligence (legal, financial, technical, customer calls)

**Step 5: Final Bids (Month 28)**
- Binding offers (LOI with terms)
- Negotiate: Valuation, structure (cash/stock), earnout, retention
- Board approval (founders + investors vote)

**Step 6: Close (Month 29-30)**
- Legal docs (purchase agreement, employment agreements)
- Regulatory approval (FTC, DOJ if >$100M)
- Wire transfer / stock issuance
- Announce publicly (press release, all-hands)

### Pros
- **Liquidity Now:** Cash out $1-2B in 6-12 months (vs 3-5 years for IPO)
- **Risk Off:** No fundraising pressure, no quarterly earnings
- **Distribution:** 100M+ developers (GitHub/MSFT reach)
- **Resources:** Unlimited budget (hire 1,000 engineers if needed)

### Cons
- **Loss of Control:** Product roadmap dictated by acquirer
- **Culture Clash:** Startup → big company (bureaucracy)
- **Earnout Risk:** 30-50% tied to 3-year performance (unvested if leave)
- **Downside Exit:** $2-3B vs $5-10B IPO (leave money on table)

### Founder Outcome (40% stake)

**Scenario A: Microsoft $4B acquisition**
- 60% cash, 40% stock
- Founders: 40% × $4B = $1.6B
- Cash: $960M (liquid immediately)
- Stock: $640M (MSFT stock, 4-year vest)
- Earnout: $400M (tied to 3-year integration goals)
- **Total potential:** $2B over 3-4 years

**Scenario B: GitHub $2B acquisition**
- 70% MSFT stock, 30% cash
- Founders: 40% × $2B = $800M
- Cash: $240M (liquid)
- Stock: $560M (MSFT stock, 4-year vest)
- Earnout: $200M (tied to GitHub Actions integration)
- **Total potential:** $1B over 3-4 years

## Decision Framework (Month 24)

### Key Questions

**1. Founder Goals?**
- Liquidity now? → Strategic acquisition ($1-2B liquid in 1 year)
- Maximize outcome? → IPO ($2B+ liquid in 3 years)
- Control/freedom? → Bootstrap ($2-4B liquid in 5+ years)

**2. Market Timing?**
- Hot market (low interest rates)? → Series C or IPO (high valuations)
- Cold market (high interest rates)? → Bootstrap or acquisition (survive)

**3. Competitive Threat?**
- GitHub Copilot adds fixing? → Series C (accelerate moat) or acquisition (exit before competition)
- No competition? → Bootstrap (take time, build defensible)

**4. Team Energy?**
- Founders burned out? → Acquisition (cash out, rest)
- Team energized? → IPO or Series C (keep building)

### Recommended Path (Month 24)

**Short-Term (Month 24-30):**
- Bootstrap to profitability (Month 30)
- Test acquisition interest (conversations, not commitment)
- Prepare for Series C (in case market heats up)

**Decision Point (Month 30):**
- Profitable + growing 50% → Stay private (bootstrap to $100M ARR)
- Profitable but slowing → Series C (accelerate growth)
- Strong acquisition offer ($3B+) → Sell (take chips off table)

**Long-Term (2030+):**
- $100M ARR + profitable → IPO ($5-10B) or strategic sale ($5-10B)
