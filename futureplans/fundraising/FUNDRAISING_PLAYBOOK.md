## Objection Handling

### "Market too crowded"
**Response:** "SonarQube/ESLint have 0% auto-fix rate. They only detect. We're the only solution that actually fixes code automatically. It's detection vs. detection + fixing - different category."

### "Too early / need more traction"
**Response:** "50 beta users validated 78% fix rate across 100K+ issues. That's product-market fit. We're not raising to find PMF - we're raising to scale GTM. $10K MRR with zero marketing spend proves demand."

### "What if GitHub builds this?"
**Response:** "GitHub tried with Copilot (60% fix rate for new code only, not existing code). We're 78% for all code. Also, we're marketplace partners - they want ecosystem, not build everything. Like Vercel on AWS."

### "Engineering risk - can you maintain quality at scale?"
**Response:** "ML model already at 92.3% accuracy on 500K training examples. Feedback loop improves with usage (data moat). Founders built similar systems at [Company X] for 5M+ users. We know how to scale."

### "Team too small for enterprise"
**Response:** "Year 1 focus: Product-led growth (GitHub Marketplace). Year 2: Developer advocates (community). Year 3: Enterprise sales team. We're hiring VP Sales in M6 with your $600K sales budget. Phased GTM, not all at once."

### "Why not raise more / Why not less?"
**Response:** "$2M gives 18-month runway to Series A at $200K MRR (highly fundable). Less = risky (12mo runway). More = unnecessary dilution (we're capital efficient: 14.7x LTV/CAC). $2M is optimal."

### "What's the moat?"
**Response:** "3 layers: (1) ML data moat (every fix improves model), (2) Patent-pending O-D-A-V-L cycle, (3) Network effects (more users ‚Üí more recipes ‚Üí higher trust scores). Defensible through data + IP + scale."

**No-Go (Walk Away):**

### Negotiation Script

**If valuation too low ($6M offer vs $10M ask):**
"We appreciate the offer, but we have [X] other term sheets at $9M-$11M. At 78% auto-fix rate and 14.7x LTV/CAC, we're best-in-class. Can you match $10M?"

"These provisions are non-standard (industry norm is [X]). Can we use [Standard VC term template]? We want founder-friendly terms that don't block growth decisions."
