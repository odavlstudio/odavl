# ODAVL Studio Data Processing Agreement (DPA)

**Agreement Type:** Data Processing Agreement (GDPR Article 28)  
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2025  
**Applicable Regulations:** GDPR, CCPA, and other applicable data protection laws

## Agreement Overview

This Data Processing Agreement ("DPA") governs the processing of personal data by ODAVL Studio ("Processor") on behalf of the customer ("Controller") in connection with the ODAVL Studio service.

This DPA supplements the Terms of Service and Service Level Agreement, and takes precedence in case of conflicts regarding data protection matters.

## Definitions

**Personal Data:** Any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person as defined under applicable data protection laws.

**Processing:** Any operation performed on personal data, including collection, storage, use, disclosure, or deletion.

**Data Subject:** An identified or identifiable natural person whose personal data is processed.

**Supervisory Authority:** The data protection authority responsible for monitoring compliance with data protection laws.

## Scope of Processing

### Categories of Data Subjects

- Customer employees and contractors
- End users of customer's applications
- Administrative contacts and billing personnel
- Technical support contacts

### Categories of Personal Data

**Account and Identity Data:**
- Names, email addresses, job titles
- Company names and contact information
- Authentication credentials and session data

**Usage and Analytics Data (Opt-in Only):**
- Feature usage patterns and preferences
- Performance metrics and error reports
- Log data and technical diagnostics

**Billing and Financial Data:**
- Payment method information (tokenized)
- Billing addresses and tax identifiers
- Transaction history and subscription details

### Processing Activities

**Service Delivery:**
- User authentication and authorization
- Service provisioning and configuration
- Usage monitoring and analytics (with consent)
- Customer support and technical assistance

**Business Operations:**
- Billing and subscription management
- Security monitoring and incident response
- Legal compliance and regulatory reporting
- Service improvement and development

## Processor Obligations

### Lawful Processing

ODAVL Studio will:

- Process personal data only on documented instructions from the Controller
- Ensure processing is lawful under applicable data protection laws
- Not process personal data for own purposes without Controller consent
- Immediately inform Controller of any unlawful processing requests

### Security Measures

**Technical Safeguards:**
- Encryption of personal data in transit (TLS 1.3) and at rest (AES-256)
- Access controls with multi-factor authentication
- Regular security monitoring and threat detection
- Secure data backup and recovery procedures

**Organizational Safeguards:**
- Staff training on data protection requirements
- Background checks for personnel with data access
- Confidentiality agreements for all staff
- Incident response and breach notification procedures

### Data Subject Rights Support

ODAVL Studio will assist Controller in fulfilling data subject rights:

**Access Rights:**
- Provide data export functionality
- Support Controller's access request responses
- Maintain records of personal data processing

**Correction and Deletion:**
- Enable Controller to update or delete personal data
- Implement automated deletion capabilities
- Provide deletion confirmation and verification

**Portability and Restriction:**
- Support data export in machine-readable formats
- Enable Controller to restrict processing activities
- Document processing restrictions and limitations

### Sub-processing

**Authorized Sub-processors:**
Current sub-processors include:

- **AWS (Amazon Web Services):** Cloud infrastructure and data storage
- **Stripe:** Payment processing and billing management
- **Plausible.io:** Website analytics (anonymized data only)

**Sub-processor Management:**
- Maintain current list of sub-processors
- 30-day advance notice for new sub-processors
- Ensure equivalent data protection obligations
- Monitor sub-processor compliance and performance

**Sub-processor Agreements:**
- Execute written agreements with equivalent protections
- Regular audit and compliance verification
- Liability for sub-processor actions and omissions

## Controller Obligations

### Processing Instructions

Controller must:

- Provide clear, documented processing instructions
- Ensure processing instructions are lawful and compliant
- Notify Processor of any instruction changes
- Maintain records of processing activities

### Data Subject Communications

Controller is responsible for:

- Providing privacy notices to data subjects
- Obtaining necessary consents for processing
- Handling data subject rights requests
- Communicating processing purposes and legal basis

## International Data Transfers

### Transfer Mechanisms

**Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs):**
- EU Commission approved SCCs incorporated by reference
- Regular monitoring of adequacy decisions
- Impact assessments for high-risk transfers

**Data Residency Options:**
- Primary data storage: United States (AWS US-East-1)
- EU data residency available for enterprise customers
- Customer control over data location preferences

### Transfer Impact Assessment

- Regular assessment of data transfer risks
- Implementation of supplementary measures when required
- Monitoring of legal developments affecting transfers
- Suspension of transfers if adequate protections unavailable

## Data Breach and Incident Response

### Breach Notification

**Internal Procedures:**
- Immediate detection and containment (within 4 hours)
- Initial assessment and classification (within 8 hours)  
- Detailed investigation and impact analysis (within 24 hours)

**Controller Notification:**
- Notify Controller within 24 hours of becoming aware of breach
- Provide detailed incident report within 72 hours
- Ongoing updates and final post-incident report
- Support Controller's regulatory notification obligations

### Breach Documentation

- Comprehensive incident log and timeline
- Evidence preservation and forensic analysis
- Root cause analysis and remediation steps
- Lessons learned and process improvements

## Data Retention and Deletion

### Retention Periods

**Account Data:**
- Active accounts: Duration of service agreement
- Terminated accounts: 90 days for account recovery
- Billing records: 7 years for tax compliance

**Usage Data:**
- Analytics data: Maximum 24 months
- Log data: 90 days for security and debugging
- Backup data: 30 days rolling retention

### Deletion Procedures

**End of Service:**
- Complete data deletion within 90 days of service termination
- Secure deletion using cryptographic erasure methods
- Deletion certification provided to Controller
- Exception: legally required retention periods

**On-Demand Deletion:**
- Controller-initiated deletion requests processed within 30 days
- Individual data subject deletion requests supported
- Verification and confirmation of successful deletion

## Audits and Compliance

### Audit Rights

Controller may:

- Request information about processing activities
- Conduct audits of Processor's data protection practices
- Engage third-party auditors with reasonable advance notice
- Review security controls and compliance measures

### Compliance Reporting

**Regular Reporting:**
- Annual compliance report with SOC 2 Type II audit
- Quarterly security and privacy metrics
- Incident reports and remediation status
- Sub-processor compliance monitoring results

**Ad-hoc Reporting:**
- Regulatory inquiry support and documentation
- Data protection authority cooperation
- Legal hold and litigation support procedures

## Liability and Indemnification

### Processor Liability

ODAVL Studio is liable for:

- Compliance with documented Controller instructions
- Implementation of appropriate security measures
- Sub-processor selection and monitoring
- Breach notification and incident response

### Limitation of Liability

- Liability limited to direct damages only
- Maximum liability: 12 months of service fees
- Exclusions for indirect, consequential, or punitive damages
- Standard commercial liability limitations apply

### Indemnification

ODAVL Studio will indemnify Controller for:

- Processor's breach of data protection obligations
- Unlawful processing by Processor or sub-processors
- Regulatory fines resulting from Processor non-compliance
- Third-party claims arising from Processor's actions

## Term and Termination

### Agreement Term

This DPA remains in effect:

- For duration of main service agreement
- Until all personal data is deleted or returned
- Through any agreement renewals or extensions

### Termination Procedures

Upon termination:

- Cease all processing except for deletion activities
- Return or delete all personal data within 90 days
- Provide deletion certification to Controller
- Maintain confidentiality obligations indefinitely
